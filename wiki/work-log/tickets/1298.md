# #1298 — Add Ed25519 cryptographic signatures to governance artifacts

## Body

## Problem

Today, governance artifacts (MANAGER_HANDOFF, COLLABORATOR_HANDOFF, ADMIN_HANDOFF, CONSULTANT_CLOSEOUT, EPIC_RESCOPE) are signed with **textual aliases** like `Signed-by: Cole Mason` followed by `Team&Model: claude-code:opus-4-7@anthropic`. Anyone can type those strings — there is no cryptographic proof the named agent actually authored the artifact.

Per [Strata 2026 — AI Agent Identity Crisis](https://www.strata.io/blog/agentic-identity/the-ai-agent-identity-crisis-new-research-reveals-a-governance-gap/), only **23% of organizations** have formal AI agent identity governance, and the resulting "non-repudiation gap" causes audit trails to trace to a human operator who didn't review the action. This is a known governance failure mode in 2026.

Epic #1271's AC6 (Consultant-only Epic close authority with cross-team signer rule) currently enforces team-attribution via the `signer_team` text field — sufficient for the harness's current trust model, but **regulatorily insufficient** under SOX/DORA/ISO 27001 organizational-independence baseline if the bar tightens (EU AI Act Art 14/26 enforcement Aug 2026; Colorado AI Act June 2026).

This Epic adds **Ed25519 cryptographic signatures** to governance artifacts, replacing text-only attestation with mathematical non-repudiation.

## Goal

Each AI agent (12 identities: 3 teams × 4 roles) holds a unique Ed25519 keypair. Every governance artifact carries a signature verifiable against the registry. Closeout-lint extends to verify signatures (not just text aliases).

## Phase-0 R&D Gate (required before implementation children)
