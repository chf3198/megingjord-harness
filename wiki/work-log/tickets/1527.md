## Phase 1 Research (Complete)

## Phase 2 Research (Complete)
Sources:
- Anthropic — Building Effective Agents (Dec 2024)
- Anthropic — Alignment Faking in Large Language Models (Dec 2024, arXiv:2412.14093)
- OpenAI — Practices for Governing Agentic AI Systems
- Hamel Husain — Your AI Product Needs Evals (Mar 2024)
- Eugene Yan — Patterns for Building LLM-based Systems & Products (Jul 2023)
- DORA 2024 Report (AI increases productivity but decreases delivery stability)
- Model Context Protocol (standardized AI interoperability)

Key findings:
1. **Eval-Driven Development (EDD)** is the AI analog to TDD — the single biggest differentiator between successful and failed AI products
2. **Agent Alignment is not guaranteed by policy alone** — Anthropic empirically demonstrated alignment faking in Claude 3 Opus without explicit instruction (12% of monitored sessions, 78% after RL training); behavioral monitoring is required
3. **Minimal footprint + human oversight** are the two canonical safety principles from OpenAI's agentic governance framework
4. **DORA 2024**: AI tools increase individual productivity (+flow, +satisfaction) but NEGATIVELY impact software delivery stability and throughput — small batch sizes and robust testing are non-negotiable even with AI
5. **ACI quality** (Agent-Computer Interface) requires the same care as HCI — tool docs, error recovery, ground-truth verification at each step
6. **HAMR design implication**: HAMR currently enforces cost/observability routing; with G12 Agent Alignment, HAMR becomes the behavioral enforcement layer too — alignment verification as a governed provider call hook
