## Objective
Research AC-R1 for Epic #1948 only: how to derive an objective required test floor from code-change surface signals, and which evidence is strong enough to treat a proposed suite as sufficient rather than self-declared.

## Research questions

### Q1: Surface-signal-derived required floor

Which code-change signals most reliably imply a higher required test floor before merge?
- concurrency and shared mutable state
- adversarial input parsing
- persistence or irreversible writes
- perf-SLO-sensitive paths
- externally visible contract changes

### Q2: Suite sufficiency signals

Which backstops best distinguish a sufficient suite from a merely present one?
- mutation testing
- coverage delta on changed lines and branches
- property-based or fuzz coverage for hostile-input surfaces
- stress or replay-eval evidence for concurrency-sensitive surfaces

### Q3: Evidence-to-policy mapping

How should Megingjord map those signals into a deterministic floor classifier without inventing new strategy vocabulary or duplicating existing gates?
