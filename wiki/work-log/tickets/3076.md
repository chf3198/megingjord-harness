## Body

Phase-1 child of #3069 (Refs #3069). Phase-0 source: #3070.

## Scope (consideration 5)
Prevent score-gaming and work-for-work's-sake; calibrate the goalpost.

## Acceptance criteria
- AC1: the rubric is owned and FROZEN by the orchestrator; the generator cannot edit it mid-loop (anti-reward-hacking, arXiv 2407.04549).
- AC2: a held-out gold anchor criterion (for example, would-this-have-prevented-the-original-incident) is scored separately and cannot be gamed by rubric softening.
- AC3: a dedicated scope-guard reviewer flags out-of-scope or over-engineering findings, which are discounted from the score; design length is controlled (verbosity-bias guard).
- AC4: stopping rule implemented — median of the capable cross-family jury reaching at least 92, OR a dry round (no new in-scope critique), OR max 3 rounds; goalpost uses the MEDIAN of capable judges, not the mean. Update review-score-contract and review-independence-promotion accordingly.
