## Body

## Problem

A recurring governance conflict exists across agent runtimes: completion-intent semantics ("complete/finish/ship" implies end-to-end baton delivery) can conflict with authorization-style constraints that prohibit commit/branch/PR operations unless interpreted as explicitly requested.

This produces cross-runtime friction and inconsistent outcomes at terminal stages:
- one runtime may proceed to admin/consultant finalization
- another runtime may halt at implementation and report "not permitted"

That ambiguity is a G1 governance defect, a G7 throughput drag, and a G9 interoperability risk.

## Why this is separate from current open work

Adjacent open issues do not directly own this precedence conflict:
- #1889 validates claim-vs-merge truth, not instruction precedence resolution.
- #2574 hardens CI completion wait behavior, not commit/branch authorization semantics.
- #2399 promotes memory rules-of-thumb, but does not define a deterministic cross-runtime precedence contract.
- #2037 is broader programmatic role automation, not this specific contradiction.

## Scope

Research-first only. This Epic starts with one planning/research child ticket and intentionally creates no AC or development children at creation time.

## Boundaries

- In scope: precedence contract design, conflict taxonomy, deterministic decision policy, cross-runtime interoperability criteria.
- Out of scope (until Phase-0 complete): validator code, hook code, workflow edits, or runtime adapter implementation.

## Expected outcome of Phase-0

A single precedence contract that all supported AI runtimes implement consistently for completion-intent and git-admin operations, plus an implementation plan that avoids policy ambiguity.
