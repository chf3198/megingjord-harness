## Body

## Objective

The #1596 red-team (Architect subagent, Copilot model) produced plausible output but operated as a raw adversarial reasoner rather than as a governed Consultant role. This Epic tracks research and hardening work to ensure every red-team/Consultant invocation — regardless of model tier (fleet or non-fleet) — consistently uses all available harness primitives.

## Problem

Post-mortem analysis of the #1596 CONSULTANT_CLOSEOUT revealed seven harness capability gaps:

1. **`rubric-score.js` not run** — deterministic v2 rubric was available but unused; legacy subjective scoring used instead (transition window expired 2026-05-22)
2. **`consultant-checks.js` not run** — 4 active FAILs (gov-005, gov-006, fleet-001, fleet-004) were never surfaced or acknowledged; mandatory output fields (`checks_run`, `checks_failed`, `remediation_issues`) absent
3. **`fleet-001` + CW-2 convergence missed** — real 429 rate-limit events exist in HAMR telemetry; the red-team's CW-2 finding was theoretical when live evidence was available from `consultant-checks.js`
4. **Wiki not searched** — 163 pages available; `[[harness-goal-controls]]`, `[[linting-governance]]`, `[[js-code-quality-practices]]` directly relevant; never consulted
5. **CLOSEOUT output contract incomplete** — 11 mandatory named fields absent (`manager_grade`, `collaborator_grade`, `admin_grade`, `drift_score`, `wiki_health`, `fleet_utilization`, `confidence`, `risk_register`, `checks_run`, `checks_failed`, `remediation_issues`)
6. **HAMR activation state not verified** — `fleet_utilization` absent; routing tier unconfirmed (agent definition specifies Opus but CLOSEOUT signed Sonnet)
7. **Signer alias conflict** — SKILL.md specifies `Quinn Critic`; canonical alias system gives `Soren Vale`; discrepancy unresolved

## Scope

This Epic covers research into and hardening of the Consultant/red-team execution process. It does NOT cover HAMR fleet model guardrail infrastructure (see companion Epic for HAMR fleet parity).
