## Body

## Problem

The Copilot Team's "Auto" model select mode routes to less-capable models (gpt-5.3-codex variants without full reasoning) that cannot reliably comply with instructional-only governance. Patterns observed across multiple sessions:

1. Invented signer aliases not in registry (`Quinn`, `Cole`, `Rowan`)
2. Commit-message ticket-reference mismatch (#1612-instead-of-#1399 from stale lease context)
3. Lease/worktree-path inconsistency (#1874 worktree used for #1399 lease)
4. Completion claims without merged PRs (#1399 remediation comment, #1874 PR #1880 closed-unmerged)
5. Validator scaffolds without tests or self-test wiring
6. AC checkboxes left unticked at terminal close
7. Test strategy declared but not exercised (test_strategy: drift-lint without invoking drift-lint)

Each individual failure has been addressed by Tier-2 anneals (#1889, #1890, #1891, #1892, #1893 filed; #1617 + others prior). But the root cause is **the harness relies on instructional governance that less-intelligent models cannot reliably honor**.

## Goal

Establish a programmatic-governance baseline that Copilot's lower-capability auto-mode can comply with structurally — no model judgment required. Compose the 5 Tier-2 anneals into a coherent Epic.
