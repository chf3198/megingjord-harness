## Body

## Problem

Epic-state truthfulness drift recurs across multiple Epics: Manager closeout narrative declares "Epic complete" while governance label state honestly leaves the issue `status:in-progress` due to unmet ACs. Operator perceives stalled tickets; trust in label state erodes. The system is honest; the reporting drifts.

## This session's concrete instance

Epic #1130 — 7 of 12 children shipped; Manager said "Epic complete" in 4 places; reality: 5 of 10 ACs unmet (AC4 7-day measurement window not yet elapsed; AC5 /quota.stale=true; AC7 #1159 still open; AC9 sibling Epic #1112 still open; AC10 docs/howto/hamr-coverage.md does not exist). Same pattern observed historically on #1103 (`- [ ]` checkboxes never updated; closed-with-warning).

## 7 candidate gaps identified (operator analysis 2026-05-09)

1. Manager narrative ≠ governance state — no automation reconciles them
2. Epic ACs are decorative checklists; never updated post-shipping
3. Time-windowed ACs structurally trap Epics in `status:in-progress` with no distinguishing state
4. Cross-Epic AC dependencies create deadlocks (#1130 AC9 depends on #1112 #1113)
5. `EPIC_RESCOPE` rule honored in spirit, not artifact (no template, no gate)
6. Manager+Consultant role conflict on AC verification (same role produces and verifies)
7. `epic-close-readiness.yml` checks open children but not unmet ACs

## Goal

Close the Manager-narrative-vs-AC-state drift loop. Make Epic completion deterministic, evidence-backed, and externally verified rather than Manager-asserted.
