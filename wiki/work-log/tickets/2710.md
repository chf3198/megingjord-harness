## Research questions (the deliverable must answer all)

- **RQ1 — Inventory**: enumerate every governance chain in the harness (baton, self-anneal, bypass-exception, state-gating, advisory-cleanup, cross-team sign-off, …) and the discrete links of each.
- **RQ2 — Classification**: classify each link as `enforced (fail-closed)` | `auto-emitted` | `operator-discretionary (BREAKABLE)`, with evidence (file/line of the enforcement point, or its absence).
- **RQ3 — Guardrail design**: for each BREAKABLE link, specify the guardrail — auto-emit hook vs fail-closed gate — and its **fail-closed + auto-queue** offline behavior (block; if unpersistable, write a local deferred marker; reconcile on reconnect). Cite the existing enforcement point it should mirror.
- **RQ4 — Self-enforcing meta-invariant**: design a machine-checkable validator that fails any NEW governance chain shipping a discretionary critical link, so the invariant cannot regress (no prose-only guarantee).
- **RQ5 — Prior art**: ground the design in current external expertise (policy-as-code admission control, fail-closed/fail-secure design, mistake-proofing/poka-yoke, durable-execution & outbox/auto-emit, tamper-evident append-only audit). Cite sources.
