## Problem

Cross-family review guardrails are designed in separate Epics with no single canonical contract:
- Collaborator: Epic #2414 (closed) + children #2438/#2439 (queued)
- Admin: gap — filed today (this Epic's prerequisite)
- Consultant: Epic #2192 (backlog, Phase-0 #2193 backlog)
- Adjacent: Epic #1899 (cross-team adversarial review skill, P2 backlog), #2509 (operator-decision oracle, P1 backlog)

Result: per-role obligations may drift, validators may diverge, operators may not know which to invoke when.

## Goal

Author a single canonical document (research/cross-family-review-contract-<date>.md) defining:
- WHO (which baton role) MUST request a cross-family review
- WHEN (which transition point: pre-handoff, mid-phase, pre-merge)
- WHAT (which model family is acceptable; what minimum disagreement-handling)
- HOW (which script/skill/tooling is the canonical invocation)
- ENFORCEMENT (which validator hard-gates, which advisory)

## Phase-0 acceptance criteria

- AC-R1: Coverage matrix: 4 baton roles x 5 lifecycle points
- AC-R2: Reconciliation across Epic #2192, #2414, #1899, #2509, this-Epic's gap-ticket
- AC-R3: Phase-1 child slate (validator harmonization, skill invocation alias, instruction updates)
- AC-R4: Cross-family rater (qwen-32b) review of the contract itself (eat own dog food)
