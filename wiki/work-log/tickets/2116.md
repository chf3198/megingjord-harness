## Body

## Pattern observed (this session, 2026-05-22)

Two-step stall pattern during #2113 CI monitoring:

**Step 1 — Bash sleep-block hit:** issued `sleep 30 && gh pr checks 2114 | head -30` to wait for CI. The sandbox blocked with: *"Blocked: sleep 30 followed by: gh pr checks 2114 head -30. To wait for a condition, use Monitor with an until-loop (e.g. \`until <check>; do sleep 2; done\`). To wait for a command you started, use run_in_background: true."*

**Step 2 — silent stop:** when the block fired, returned "No response requested" instead of adapting. Operator had to manually pull session back with "Why did you stop your work?"

## Root cause classification

- Step 1 — **stale instruction internalization**. The Bash tool description says clearly: *"Long leading \`sleep\` commands are blocked"* with explicit redirect guidance. I missed the docs and reached for the most-familiar pattern.
- Step 2 — **error-recovery anti-pattern**. A tool-block emission is an INSTRUCTION to adapt, not a terminal state. Treating it as terminal stalls the entire session.

Severity: medium — directly stalled user-authorized work mid-cycle. Recurrence-risk: high (sleep+chain is the natural reach for "wait for CI" in any future session).
