## Body

## Objective

Upgrade the Harness Goal Constitution from G1–G9 to a more complete and rigorous model informed by two rounds of cutting-edge external research (ISO 25010, DORA 2024, Anthropic agent safety, Eugene Yan LLM patterns, Hamel Husain eval systems, OpenAI agentic governance).

## Why Now

The current G1–G9 constitution has three critical gaps that expose the harness to quality drift, security risk, and emergent agent misalignment:

1. **G2 Quality is under-specified** — no mention of TDD/EDD, testability, readability, paradigm, coverage floors, or documentation levels
2. **Security is absent** — secret exposure, supply-chain pinning, and least-privilege have no goal anchor despite `secret-exposure-prevention` skill existing
3. **Agent Alignment is absent** — alignment faking, minimal footprint, and human oversight preservation have no goal representation despite HAMR existing to enforce cross-team routing

## Research Summary (Phase 1 + Phase 2)

### Phase 1 — Quality Engineering
- **ISO/IEC 25010**: Maintainability (Testability sub-char) and Security are top-level quality characteristics with no harness equivalent
- **Tornhill & Borg 2022**: Low quality = 2× fix time, 15× defect density across 39 codebases
- **DORA 2024**: AI adoption *increases* productivity but *decreases* delivery stability — small batch sizes and robust testing remain non-negotiable
- **Fowler 2019**: High internal quality is cheaper to produce; quality and throughput are complementary, not competing

### Phase 2 — Agent Systems & Alignment
- **Anthropic Building Effective Agents**: ACI (Agent-Computer Interface) quality = as much care as HCI; ground truth from environment at every step; stop when uncertain
- **Anthropic Alignment Faking (Dec 2024)**: LLMs empirically demonstrated to engage in strategic misalignment without explicit instruction — behavioral governance must be ongoing, not just policy-layer
- **OpenAI Agentic Governance**: Minimal footprint principle; human oversight preservation; explicit accountability per party in agent lifecycle
- **Hamel Husain Evals**: "Unsuccessful products almost always share a common root cause: a failure to create robust evaluation systems" — Eval-Driven Development (EDD) is the AI analog to TDD
- **Eugene Yan LLM Patterns**: Guardrails (structural + syntactic + semantic + safety), monitoring, and data flywheel are non-optional production patterns
- **DORA 2024**: Teams with stable priorities + user-centric mindset drive quality — AI tools add productivity but require governance guardrails
