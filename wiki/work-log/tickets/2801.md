## Body

## Problem (self-anneal — process gap surfaced 2026-06-09)

The `/research-redteam-loop` (and the research-first Epic consensus gate) reaches a >93 consensus
on a *design* **without any corpus overlap/conflict/redundancy/gap analysis** against existing
open/closed tickets and shipped work. The cross-family reviewers are **stateless** (no repo access),
so they cannot perform this check; and the Manager's 'Non-redundancy' claim in the deliverable is
**self-asserted and never independently validated**.

## Concrete near-miss (evidence)
Epic #2791 / Phase-0 #2792 reached **96/ACCEPT** while **#2518** (closed Epic: *cross-team fleet
utilization, patience policy, and shared observability*) was never analyzed — despite real overlap
with the design's cost-aware routing (D1) and observability (#2800). The Manager's manual scan
missed it; the red-team had no mechanism to catch it.

## Relationship to existing work (non-redundant — boundary recorded)
- **#2617** (closed) enforces `related_tickets:`/`overlap_decision:` at **ticket creation** —
  a different lifecycle stage. **#2623** owns its enforcement rollout.
- This ticket covers the **research/design-consensus stage**: it REUSES #2617's overlap machinery
  (deterministic semantic scan + the two fields) but applies it inside the red-team loop and makes
  the overlap finding part of what the cross-family reviewers validate. No duplication of #2617.

## Acceptance criteria
- AC1: `/research-redteam-loop` gains a mandatory **corpus-overlap stage** before ACCEPT: run the
  #2617 deterministic semantic-overlap scan over the design's claimed scope; produce a
  `related_tickets:` + `overlap_decision:` block in the deliverable.
- AC2: the overlap findings are **fed into the reviewer prompt** so the cross-family red-team
  validates the boundary (overlap vs conflict vs redundancy vs gap), not just design quality.
- AC3: ACCEPT is **blocked** when overlap is high and no explicit boundary decision is recorded
  (mirrors #2617's handoff block, at the consensus gate).
- AC4: tests cover: missed-overlap is caught; recorded-boundary passes; gap-vs-redundancy classified.
