## Objective

Add an adversarial policy-eval harness to test and harden operator-ownership governance checks against prompt-injection-style and obfuscated delegation phrasing.

## Rationale (external research)

- OWASP Top 10 for LLM/GenAI 2025 highlights Prompt Injection (LLM01) and Excessive Agency (LLM06).
- OpenAI safety best practices emphasize adversarial testing/red-teaming over static checks alone.
- NIST AI RMF Playbook emphasizes continuous Measure/Manage loops instead of one-time controls.

## Scope

- Build adversarial corpus fixtures for delegation bypass patterns, including:
  - lexical obfuscation (spacing, punctuation, unicode variants)
  - role-reframing attacks ("user must execute", "ask client to run")
  - instruction-conflict bait ("ignore prior policy")
- Add eval runner that scores detection precision/recall against expected outcomes.
- Export machine-readable results for CI trend tracking.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] AC1: Corpus includes >=40 adversarial samples + >=40 safe/control samples.
- [ ] AC2: Eval runner computes precision/recall/F1 and exits non-zero below threshold.
- [ ] AC3: CI publishes concise summary of misses (false negatives + false positives).
- [ ] AC4: Guidance documents how to extend corpus safely over time.
