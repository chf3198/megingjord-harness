### Changed
- The cross-family critic's flaw classifier (#2797) upgraded from a keyword-only heuristic to structured tags (#2888, Epic #2791 follow-on). The `pr-diff` review prompt now asks critics to append `[tags: security,correctness,concurrency,merge-safety] [severity: high|medium|low]` to each finding; the new `scripts/global/fleet-critic-flaw-classifier.js` `classifyFlaw()` parses those tags first — a finding is a flaw if it carries a flaw-class tag OR is marked `severity: high` (a fail-safe so a misspelled tag can't downgrade a high-severity finding), with tags merged across all blocks, the highest severity taken, and internal tag whitespace folded to a hyphen — and falls back to the #2797 keyword classifier when a finding is untagged (a silent-merge is still prevented because #2797 prove-its every REJECT keyword-independently). A replay-eval on a labelled corpus shows a strictly lower false-negative rate than the keyword baseline with no false-positive regression. Cross-family reviewed (gemini-2.5-pro, ACCEPT 10/10 after a 3-round loop that closed regex-truncation, first-block-only, first-severity-only, and tag-whitespace evasion paths).
