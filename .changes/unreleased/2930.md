### Changed
- Availability-vs-capability escalation with a circuit-breaker (#2930, Epic #2926 C4): fleet escalation is now governed by `scripts/global/fleet-escalation-policy.js`, which encodes the #2619 cost-ascending mandate as a hard rule — an **availability failure (outage) never escalates to a paid premium tier**, it fails over to the free $0 cloud; only a **capability failure at the free-cloud tier** may reach premium. A shared circuit-breaker (`circuit-breaker.js`, 5-fail / 30s cooloff) lets `cascade-dispatch` skip the fleet attempt entirely once it is known-down (no probe+timeout cost on every call during an outage), and resets on a fleet success. `cascade-dispatch.escalationTier` now delegates to the policy (back-compat preserved). The D4 metric **premium-on-outage = 0** is asserted by a 500-iteration stress chaos test. G3 (outage stays $0) + G6 (resilience).
